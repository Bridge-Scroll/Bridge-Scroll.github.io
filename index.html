What is Bridge Scroll?

Scroll Bridge is the Scroll portal flow for moving supported assets between Ethereum mainnet and Scroll, a native zkEVM Layer 2 for Ethereum; ethereum.org's blockchain bridge overview explains why bridges are used to transfer assets and information between separate blockchain ecosystems.

Bridge to Scroll from Ethereum

Bridging to Scroll from Ethereum moves ETH or supported ERC-20 assets from Ethereum mainnet to the Scroll zkEVM network. Users connect a wallet, select Ethereum as source, choose Scroll as destination, review the token and fees, then sign the deposit. After funds arrive, Scroll transactions use ETH for gas. The official Scroll bridge guide is the primary reference for supported assets, wallet state, and bridge status. Third-party bridges may offer different assets or faster routes, but their risk and fee models are separate.

Scroll bridge withdrawal time and finality

Scroll withdrawals from L2 to Ethereum are slower than simple deposits because they rely on the network's L2-to-L1 withdrawal process and proof/finality steps. The exact timing can vary with network conditions and bridge design. Fast third-party bridges may provide liquidity-based exits, but they add provider fees and liquidity risk; Binance Academy's blockchain bridge explainer outlines how bridge mechanisms and risks differ. Users comparing routes should check whether the transaction is an official bridge withdrawal or a fast bridge transfer, the destination token, and the final received amount.

Scroll bridge fees and gas token

Scroll bridge cost depends on direction, source-chain gas, destination-chain gas, and any provider fee. Depositing from Ethereum normally requires Ethereum gas, while activity on Scroll uses ETH as the gas token. If a bridge route includes a swap or third-party liquidity provider, the quote can also include spread, bridge fees, and slippage; DeFiLlama's Scroll chain metrics page is a useful independent place to compare network-level DeFi activity without relying on a static snapshot. Keep ETH on Scroll for approvals, swaps, and withdrawals.

Bridge Scroll FAQ

What is the official Scroll bridge?

The official Scroll bridge is available through the Scroll portal and is used to move supported assets between Ethereum and Scroll. The Scroll bridge docs are the safest starting point for checking the correct domain, supported assets, and bridge status. Other bridge providers may support Scroll, but they are separate services with their own liquidity, fees, and risk assumptions.

What token do I need for gas on Scroll?

Scroll uses ETH for gas. After bridging to Scroll, keep a small amount of ETH on the Scroll network so you can approve tokens, swap, interact with apps, claim assets, or start a withdrawal; Chainlist's Scroll mainnet network details help verify chain ID and explorer information. ETH on Ethereum mainnet cannot pay Scroll gas until it is deposited or otherwise moved to Scroll.

Can I bridge USDC to Scroll?

USDC support depends on the bridge route and token representation. Some routes may support USDC directly, while others may use bridged versions or require a swap. Before bridging USDC to Scroll, use a market-data source such as CoinMarketCap's Scroll listing for token context, then check the token contract and whether the destination app or exchange supports that exact version. Stablecoin names can look identical while representing different contracts.

Why is my Scroll bridge withdrawal taking so long?

Scroll withdrawals to Ethereum may take longer because L2-to-L1 exits involve proof and finality steps rather than an instant transfer. L2BEAT's Scroll scaling profile gives independent context for the network's rollup design. If it is through a fast bridge, use that provider's tracker because delays may relate to liquidity, relayers, or a claim step.

Does Scroll have a token?

Scroll has the SCR ecosystem token, but ETH is the gas token used for Scroll network transactions. Users do not need SCR to pay normal gas fees. CoinGecko's SCR market-data page tracks the token separately from ETH gas usage, while SCR may be relevant for ecosystem participation, governance, or app-specific uses.

How do I add Scroll to MetaMask?

Most users add Scroll by connecting through the official Scroll portal, a trusted app prompt, or wallet network discovery. Confirm the network name, chain ID, RPC, and explorer against wallet instructions such as MetaMask's network management guide before approving a custom network. Once added, the wallet must hold ETH on Scroll to pay gas for any transaction.
